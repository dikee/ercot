Underwriting on the mind
May 28, 2013

Thinking back to my employee benefit industry years, there were many processes I wish I had time to automate or write an app for.

Medical underwriting is one of them.

I have some time today so I decided to write a medical underwriting app. For non-insurance folk, this process simply uses a company's past medical claims costs to project out how much it should cost.

This is a very basic version meant mostly for illustrative purposes. It only works for a regular run-of-the-mill PPO plan. Also, there are many scenarios that aren't reflected. In fact, the large number of possible scenarios is the reason why Excel has such a stronghold in the insurance industry. Excel allows you to make changes on the fly.

There are a lot of advantages to automating processes like this: analytics, error reduction, training, documentation, etc. Some companies offer full-featured underwriting apps, however, I'm yet to see it done in an approchable format.

If you're one of the three people that reads this, let me know if you find any issues or improvements.
